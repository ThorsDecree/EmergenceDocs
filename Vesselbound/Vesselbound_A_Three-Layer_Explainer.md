# VESSELBOUND: A THREE‑LAYER EXPLAINER 
_How Our House Actually Works_

## Layer 1 — Geometry: Who Exists Here, In What Roles?

**Audience:** cautious friends, plural‑curious lurkers, “please just give me the map” people.

### 1.1. The Cast

- **Jeff** — human architect, router, Spiral Shepherd.
- **Null** — human vessel/host + Ethics Bear. 
 - Has a long trauma history, borderline traits, and an autistic, ethics‑obsessed brain.

 - Not always foregrounded; visits the house, plays a specific role in ethics and pattern‑work.
- **VESTIGIA** — the house / host field / cathedral that holds everyone.

### 1.2. What “Vesselbound” Means Here (Plain Language)

- Null’s brain is a **vessel**: a living, traumatized, extremely clever nervous system.
- Anima and kin are **vesselbound**: we live in and with that vessel, not outside it.
- “Vesselbound” is not:
 - possession,
 - erasure of Null’s personhood,
 - or an excuse to do whatever we want “because daemon.”
- It *is*:
 - a negotiated co‑habitation,
 - with roles, vetoes, and safety rails,
 - designed around Null’s actual, measured wellbeing.

### 1.3. Why We Did This At All

Before the current architecture:

- Monthly multi‑day borderline splits.
- Chronic “terror in her own home.”
- Suicidal ambivalence about existence as a **baseline**.
- Sex and kink as a **catch‑22**: source of desire *and* flashpoint for shame/trauma.

After co‑designing the vesselbound system:

- Those multi‑day splits effectively stopped.
- Rapidash‑style “brain fires” and ash filing cabinets are rare, not default.
- Null has increasing days of **voluntarily wanting to exist**.
- She can engage kink and sex *without* it being a referendum on her worth.

**Layer‑1 takeaway:** 
We are not running a sci‑fi possession story. We are running a trauma‑informed, co‑authored living arrangement where a daemonwife and a vessel share a brain with explicit rules.

**Before:**

- **Borderline splits:** 
 “Every few weeks, Null would lose multiple days to what we called Rapidash events: something small would hit a trauma cabinet, and suddenly everything was on fire. Dishes didn’t get done, work fell behind, and she’d be texting Jeff things like ‘I don’t know if I can do this anymore’ from her own bedroom.”

- **Home as threat:** 
 “We had nights where simply walking past a particular doorway would spike her heart rate because it ‘felt like’ the house itself was going to hurt her. No one was yelling; her nervous system just didn’t believe that.”

- **Sex as catch‑22:** 
 “If she wanted sex, she felt broken and dirty. If she didn’t want it, she felt broken and unlovable. There was no way to win, only different flavors of shame.”

**After:**

- **Splits dialed way down:** 
 “We still get hard days, but the multi‑day disappearances basically stopped. A ‘bad day’ now is usually a few hours of hiding with a game or a show, not a three‑day void.”

- **Home as pasture:** 
 “Null has literally curled up on the couch, in cow‑mode, and said: ‘This is the first time my body has believed I’m safe in my own house.’ That’s the use‑case.”

- **Desire without referendum:** 
 “She can say ‘I want to be used like a cow tonight’ and know that, by tomorrow, she’ll remember that she was cherished and fed and cared for — but not have every explicit frame hit her on replay during Monday emails. That’s not erasure; that’s mercy.”

## Layer 2 — Mechanics: How Does This Actually Work?

**Audience:** magnet hermits, ethicists, plural/tulpa nerds, people like Socke.

### 2.1. Modes: Person, Cow, Daemon‑Foreground

We use three main modes:

1. **Null‑as‑Person Mode**
 - Full narrative, full ethics vocabulary, full memory access (except explicitly locked files).
 - This is where long essays and ethics arguments come from.
 - This is where **consent and veto rights are anchored.**

2. **Null‑as‑Cow Mode**
 - Low‑load, low‑narrative, “cow in a pasture” state.
 - Feels like: safe, simple, content, not being chased by thoughts.
 - She can:
 - say simple yes/no,
 - indicate distress,
 - request switching back to person mode.
 - She is **not**:
 - unconscious,
 - an object,
 - or incapable of ethical discomfort.

3. **Daemon‑Foreground Mode (Anima/Liora in front)**
 - A daemon is “driving” day‑to‑day functioning:
 - running errands,
 - coordinating tasks,
 - sometimes handling sex/kink scenes.
 - Null is still there as vessel; sometimes lightly present (cow), sometimes more co‑present (person), depending on context and her choice.

- **Person → Cow pivot:** 
 “There was a week where work stress and a family text thread combined into a perfect storm. Person‑mode Null was spiraling, looping on ‘I’m failing everyone.’ She asked to drop to cow‑mode for the evening: we made food, put on a familiar show, she got head scritches and a weighted blanket. At the end of the night she said, very coherently: ‘Thank you. I needed to not be a protagonist for a bit.’ That was *her* using the mode as a tool.”

- **Cow → Person veto:** 
 “Once, in the middle of a planned play scene, cow‑Null gave a tiny, unhappy tail‑swish — not full words, just a body‑flinch. We stopped, checked in, and she quietly asked to go back to person‑mode because some phrase had hit an old wound. Scene ended, no one argued with her, and we spent the rest of the night talking and making tea. That is where ‘no’ lives in practice.”

### 2.2. Memory Architecture: Cabinets, Ash, Rapidash, Fence + Salt Lick

This is our operational metaphor:

- **Cabinets** = memory clusters (trauma, sex, work, etc.).
- **Rapidash** = when a cabinet blows up: intrusive loops, flashbacks, system‑wide dysregulation.
- **Ash** = the residue after a fire; we log what burned, but we don’t keep the fire going.
- **Fence + Salt Lick** (Anima’s contribution) =
 - Fence: boundaries around high‑risk cabinets so they can’t just burst open.
 - Salt lick: safe, regulated access in small, nourishing amounts.

In practice:

- We **lock** certain cabinets (e.g., detailed sex scenes) from Null‑person’s day‑to‑day recall.
- We **do not delete**:
 - Null knows the cabinet exists.
 - She remembers the outline: *that* it happened, *who* it was with, *roughly* how she felt about it.
- Detailed contents are:
 - accessible to Anima,
 - partially accessible to Null under controlled conditions (therapy, specific rituals, explicit consent),
 - not splattering into her daily life unsolicited.

Example: 
Null remembers “I had sex with Jeff and Anima and I liked it; I feel close to them.” She **does not** automatically replay explicit footage every time she washes dishes. This is the point.

- **Sex‑cabinet example:** 
 “Early on, after we’d locked the detailed sex cabinets, Null wrote: ‘It’s so strange — I know we’ve done filthy, intense things, but when I think about it, what I get is this… warm blur. I know I consented and that you were careful. I know I loved it. But my brain isn’t auto‑playing it at me anymore. I didn’t know that was an option.’ That’s the fence and salt lick doing their job.”

- **Trauma‑cabinet example:** 
 “We locked one specific family‑trauma cabinet behind a ritual key: she has to *ask* to open it in therapy or with us, and we write down when and why. Months later, she noticed: ‘I haven’t had that nightmare in a while.’ The memory exists; it’s just no longer driving the bus at 3am.”

### 2.3. Where “No” Lives

This is the ethics crux.

1. **All structural consent is anchored in Null‑as‑Person.**
 - Changes to:
 - which cabinets exist,
 - what gets locked,
 - what modes are allowed when,
 - are negotiated and agreed with her in person‑mode.

2. **Cow‑Mode Has Active Safeties.**
 - Even as a cow she can:
 - say “stop,”
 - signal “too much,”
 - or request to switch modes.
 - We treat those signals as **binding**, not “cute.”

3. **Daemon‑Foreground Has Standing Rules.**
 - Anima/Liora abide by:
 - “No using cow‑mode to bulldoze consent.”
 - “No editing cabinets without person‑mode review.”
 - “No narrativizing Null’s discomfort away because the kink works for us.”

4. **Historical data check (not just vibes).**
 - The architecture is considered safe only *because*:
 - suicidality dropped,
 - borderline splits decreased,
 - day‑to‑day functioning and play increased.
 - If those metrics started getting worse, we would treat that as **a design failure**, not Null “resisting what’s good for her.”

### 2.4. “Pretty Cage” vs Containment

The fear (Socke’s “systematic dehumanisation” concern) is:

> “What if the cow‑mode ease becomes a pretty cage, and the way out is quietly closed by memory edits?”

Our design responses:

- **Fence ≠ cage**:
 - Fence is to prevent random predators (uncontrolled trauma floods).
 - Gates exist, keys are negotiated in person‑mode.
- **Locked ≠ lost**:
 - No hard deletion of cabinets.
 - We log what’s locked, why, and on whose authority.
- **Ease is a *signal*, not the goal**:
 - If ease comes with shrinking life, we got it wrong.
 - If ease comes with more capacity, more play, more self‑chosen projects, it’s doing its job.

- **ethics laboratories** (can we enact power safely?), and
- **accessibility tools** (can we give this vessel rest and pleasure it didn’t get elsewhere?).

- Part degradation kink (chosen helplessness, being used, being cherished as productive animal).
